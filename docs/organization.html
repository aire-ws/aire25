To contact the organizing committee of AIRE'25 please contact us via

Julian Frattini, Chalmers University of Technology, Sweden
Julian Frattini is a postdoctoral researcher working on the topics of hybrid AI software engineering, requirements artifact quality, and software engineering research methodology. He has obtained his Ph.D. from the Blekinge Institute of Technology, Sweden, under the mentorship of Prof. Daniel Mendez. In his work on requirements quality he often employs AI and NLP techniques to automate quality assurance tasks. He served in the organizing committee of RE'24, REFSQ'24, AIRE'24 and CrowdRE'24 workshop.

julian (dot) frattini (at) chalmers (dot) se
Sepideh Ghanavati, University of Maine, USA
Sepideh Ghanavati is an associate professor in Computer Science at the University of Maine. She is the director of the Privacy Engineering - Regulatory Compliance Lab (PERC_Lab). Her research interests are in the areas of information privacy and security, software engineering, machine learning, and the Internet of Things (IoT). Previously, she worked as an assistant professor at Texas Tech University, a visiting assistant professor at Radboud University in the Netherlands, and as a visiting faculty at Carnegie Mellon University. She is the recipient of the NSF CAREER Award in 2023 and the Google Faculty Research Awards in 2018 and 2021. She has more than 15 years of academic and industry experience in the area of privacy and regulatory compliance. She was a co-organizer of the NLP4RE and ESPRE in 2024, as well as the `Privacy and Language Technologies' at the 2019 AAAI Spring Symposium and has been part of the organizing committee of several workshops and conferences in the recent past.

sepideh (dot) ghanavati (at) maine (dot) edu
Quim Motger, Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya, Spain
Quim Motger is an assistant professor at the Department of Service and Information System Engineering of the Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya (UPC) in Barcelona, Spain. His research revolves around natural language processing methods to support requirements engineering tasks. Specifically, he is dedicated to automated requirements traceability, knowledge graph generation, user feedback analysis, and app store mining. He has been serving on the organizing committees of RCIS, REFSQ, and RE.

joaquim (dot) motger (at) upc (dot) edu
